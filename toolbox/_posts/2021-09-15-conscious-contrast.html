These techniques are useful for drawing the viewer's attention to a certain part of the screen, then guide them to more information. Another way to put it, "use contrast to get attention, use copy to change what the viewer knows".

Example of "consciously use contrast"

The red amount is just enough contrast to tell you something is wrong. The designer consciously chose to not make the other amounts green, which would be contrast overload.

Example of "least effective difference"

The highlighted "Bedroom" tab catches the eye. The gray color of the neighboring tabs is the least difference that can be made to distinguish the active and inactive tabs.
