I have been reading this book off and on for the past year or so.

Favorite quotes

"...design will always be about creating something that does not yet exist".

"Design is about service on behalf of the other"

In my words

Regarding the first quote: Science describes "what is" or "the true" whereas design creates what "should be", making it a matter of will and intention.

Regarding the second quote: as designers, we're not forcing our will upon others or merely helping clients create what they already know they want. Ideally, we're co-designing with clients to produce the "expected unexpected", i.e. something that the client could not have imagined and realized on their own, yet resonates with their desire.

Get the book The Design Way, Intentional Change in an Unpredictable World - by Harold G. Nelson and Erik Stolterman
