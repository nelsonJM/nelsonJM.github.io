Video Assessment Tool: Designing the Commenting capability

I decided to make a small challenge for myself to demonstrate my design process/thinking/approach to help give you a better understanding of my capability.

I found the video assessment tool while learning more about tk20 at tk20.com.

Below are the steps I went through to arrive at the design.

Model the domain

Taking information from this page, I constructed a simple model of the domain. I listed out the concepts and terms in no particular order:

  • time stamps
  • evaluator
  • student
  • comment
  • shared video
  • etc...

Sketching out flows

Next, I sketched out the flow for adding a comment to a video and for sharing a video (although I restricted my focus to the commenting capability).

Flow for Adding a Comment: When an evaluator adds a comment, they will be taken to an "add a comment" state where they have the option to create a comment and save or cancel. In this state, they can also see a preview of the video which shows them the time stamp for their comment. After saving a new comment, the evaluator is taken back to the video watching state and the new comment appears below the video.

Flow for Sharing: When an evaluator decides to share a video (by clicking a link or button), they will be taken to a 'sharing' view where they will be able to choose with whom they would like to share via email. Possible methods of selecting email recipients include:

  • select from contacts list
  • select from suggested/frequent contacts
  • manually enter email addresses of select persons

The flow continues with recipients receiving an email with a link to the video and comments.

The Capabilities: Commenting and Sharing

Commenting and sharing are two of the core capabilities that the tool offers evaluators. It is composed of:

  • ability to create and save comments
  • preview location of their comment via a video preview
  • the recently created comment appears below the video
