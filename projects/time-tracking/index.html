Time-tracking App: Design & Prototype

My contribution: Design

Year(s): 2016

The Case Study Structure

This case study is built around the following layers of design:

Outcome

Defining the intended outcome of the project, the problem being solved.

System

Defining the components of the system and how they are related, the features required

Interaction

Defining how people will interact with the components.

Visual Design

Defining layout, typography, iconography, color.

Outcome

A prototype for a time-tracking app that is focused on fast, after-the-fact, time entry.

This is a time-tracking app for individuals and teams to track their time spent on projects.

It's not easy to remember to track my time at the outset of a project-related task. I am usually working on several projects throughout my day, switching from task to task and stopping mid-task to resolve the occasional emergency. Ideally, I would say something like "Ok, I'm now going to work on project B, so I'll begin tracking my time", but in reality, I just do my work and record my time later.

The following are capabilities the app will offer:

  • Add time to a project for "today" and any given day.
  • See how much time I spent on a project.
  • See how much time my team has spent on a project.
  • See how I spent my time this month.

System

I began with the question, "How do I add time?" and began sketching UI flows.

By asking this question, I had a starting point and an action that came to mind. I knew there was some screen where I could select a particular project and add time to the project. I began to sketch the flow for selecting a project, a specific date and finally entering the time spent on that project.

Interaction

The flows lead to a system of features which I composed into a prototype.

View Prototype: InVision prototype

The affordance for adding/subtracting time is a "scrubber" similar to those found on audio apps like SoundCloud.
